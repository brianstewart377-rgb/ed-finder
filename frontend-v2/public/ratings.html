How Systems Are Rated

ED Finder scores every populated-eligible system out of 100 based on how good a colony it would make. Here's exactly what goes into the number.

The score in one paragraph

Each system gets a score from 0–100. Per the Colonisation Mega-Guide v2.3, stations inherit the top two economies of their parent body, so the score is built around complementary economy pairs — not single economies. We score each economy from your system's bodies, find the strongest game-supported pair, blend in your top-3 average, and apply a rarity gate so 90+ requires genuinely rare features.

The complementary-pair list

Your system's pair score is the average of the two economies in its strongest game-supported pair. The list is exactly the productive pairings from the Mega-Guide:

  • Extraction + Refinery — mining stack (Rocky + Ring), the gold standard
  • Refinery + Agriculture — Rocky + Bio, "best farm in the game"
  • Extraction + Industrial — HMC + Geo, mining-to-manufacturing
  • Industrial + HighTech — tech production
  • HighTech + Tourism — exotic destination (ELW, near BH/NS)
  • Agriculture + Tourism — Water World leisure
  • HighTech + Military — arsenal
  • Industrial + Military — defence-production
  • Agriculture + HighTech — bio-research (ELW with farm)

Note: Refinery + Industrial is deliberately not a complementary pair — Mega-Guide Appendix D shows they cannibalize each other's markets across nearly every commodity. If your Rocky+Geo body produces both, the recommended play is to "flip" toward Refinery + Extraction or Industrial + Military.

The formula

score = best_pair_avg × 0.60
      + top_3_economy_avg × 0.35
      + strategic_bonus  (max +7, from terraforming + body diversity)
      + rocky_or_hmc_bio_bonus  (+2 if applicable)

then apply CEILINGS based on rarity:
Standout countCeiling behaviour
0Compress 80–100 → 80–84 (great mining is "great", not unicorn)
1Compress 87–100 → 87–92 (one rare feature)
2+No compression — these are the genuine unicorns

Plus slot-floor gating, since per the Mega-Guide a system with fewer than 10 landable + orbital slots can't support enough Tier-3 ports to reach top-band economy:

  • < 10 slots: ceiling soft-capped at 75 + (raw − 75) × 0.40
  • 10–19 slots: ceiling soft-capped at 85 + (raw − 85) × 0.60
  • 20+ slots: no slot-cap

Standout features

"Standouts" are scarcity signals that justify a 90+ score. We weight them by how rare and impactful they are:

  • Earth-Like World = +2 (population cap 3.1 B)
  • Water World = +1 (population cap 1.8 B)
  • Ammonia World = +1 (exotic-rare)
  • Black Hole / Neutron Star = +1 each (Tourism specialty)
  • 2+ Water Worlds in one system = +1 extra
  • 5+ terraformable bodies = +1 (long-term colony potential)

What the score is *not*

  • Not a measure of current economy. ED Finder rates systems for colonisation potential — what they could become with a good build, not what they are now.
  • Not Frontier-canonical. This is a community-built scoring system. Frontier doesn't expose a "system colonisation rating" anywhere in-game.
  • Not a substitute for visual inspection. A 95 system might still not match your aesthetic preferences — go fly to it!

Calibration anchors

Sample systems and how they should score under v3.4:

  • System with 5 HMCs, 36 plain landables, 0 ELW/WW/AW, 0 exotics → ~80–84 (great mining, no unicorn factor)
  • System with 1 ELW + ringed Rocky + 5 terraformable HMCs → ~88–92
  • System with 2 ELW + WW + Ammonia + Neutron → ~95–100 (generational)
  • System with 1 G-star and 0 useful bodies → ~3–8
