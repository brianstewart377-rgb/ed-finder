# Rating System Current Contract

## What `score` means

The overall `score` (0–100) represents **best-build potential** — the theoretical ceiling for a colony build in this system given its body mix, economy fit, strategic value, and slot potential. It does **not** mean the system is universally excellent; it means the optimal build archetype could achieve this level of colony quality.

- The score is generated offline by `build_ratings.py` and stored in the `ratings` table.
- It is based on body counts, body types, signal counts, star type, and modifiers.
- It does **not** incorporate player choices, actual build plans, or simulation results.

## Economy scores

Each economy (Agriculture, Refinery, Industrial, HighTech, Military, Tourism, **Extraction**) receives a 0–100 suitability score based on how well the system's body mix supports that economy's mechanics.

- Extraction was added in rating v3.1 and must be shown in the UI alongside the other six.
- The suggested economy (`economy_suggestion`) is the highest-scoring economy.
- Rating v3.4 attenuates cross-economy saturation: the top two economies keep their raw score, the third is reduced, and the fourth-plus are reduced further. Current recalculations should not produce four or more simultaneous 100 scores.
- Existing rows with `rating_version IS NULL` may still carry pre-v3.4 saturated scores until they are rebuilt.

## Top complementary pair

The `score_breakdown.top_pair` field identifies the two economies that work best together in this system:

```json
{
  "a": "Refinery",
  "b": "Industrial",
  "a_score": 78,
  "b_score": 60,
  "pair_score": 82
}
```

The pair score represents the combined suitability ceiling when both economies are targeted in a build plan.

## Confidence

Confidence (0.70–1.00) reflects data freshness:
- Recent EDDN scans with multiple reports → higher confidence
- Old Spansh dump data with single reports → lower confidence

The frontend shows High (≥75%), Medium (≥50%), Low (<50%), or "Confidence unknown" when missing.

## Rationale

A one-line text explainer (≤160 chars) generated by `generate_rationale()` in `build_ratings.py`. Designed to be shown directly in the UI. May reference body types and counts that contributed to the score.

## Score dimensions (breakdown)

Stored in `score_breakdown.dimensions`:

| Dimension | What it measures |
|---|---|
| slots | Available orbital + surface slot capacity |
| strategic | Star type, body quality, signal density |
| safety | Orbital safety factors |
| terraforming | Terraforming potential (ELW/WW/ammonia/terraformable counts) |
| diversity | Body type diversity (Shannon entropy) |

## Stale rating / rebuild limitation

- Ratings are built offline by `build_ratings.py` and stored in the `ratings` table with an `updated_at` timestamp.
- `rating_version` is stored in the `ratings` table and mirrored to the API. Current `build_ratings.py` writes `3.4`.
- The `confidence` field reflects data freshness, not rating algorithm version.
- If a rating appears stale, has `rating_version = NULL`, or shows three-plus capped economy scores, the operational remedy is to re-run `build_ratings.py` for the affected systems.
- The frontend shows conservative wording ("Best-build potential") and a caveat for saturated/stale economy scores rather than definitive claims.

## Frontend display contract

The `RatingRadar` component must show:
1. **Headline**: "Best-build potential" with overall score
2. **Confidence**: tier badge or "Confidence unknown"
3. **Score explanation**: "Based on body mix, economy fit, strategic value, and slot potential."
4. **Top pair**: when `score_breakdown.top_pair` exists
5. **Primary/secondary economy**: from breakdown or system fields
6. **Rationale**: when available
7. **Economy suitability bars**: all 7 economies including Extraction
8. **Radar chart**: 7-axis polygon
9. **Expandable dimension breakdown**: when `score_breakdown.dimensions` exists
